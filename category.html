Climate Change

Climate change is a crucial issue in the sector of environmental protection. The cause of climate change is attributed to human activities, like burning fossil fuels, which release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. These gases disturb the natural climate system of the earth and increase surface temperature. The consequences include increasing global temperatures, melting glaciers, and rising sea levels, posing a significant threat to ecosystems and human well-being.

Pollution

Pollution is a multifaceted environmental issue that includes air, water, and soil pollution, each arising from various human activities. Air pollution is primarily caused by emissions from cars, industrial processes, and burning fossil fuels. Water pollution can come from agricultural runoff, industrial discharges, and improper waste disposal. Soil pollution results from the use of pesticides, industrial waste, and deforestation. These actions degrade land and food quality, posing a significant threat to ecosystems, human health, and animals worldwide.

Deforestation

Deforestation arises from several human activities, such as logging, agriculture, and urbanization, contributing to the excessive cutting of trees. This affects the balance of ecosystems, leading to habitat loss for animals and a decrease in biodiversity. Trees generate oxygen, retain moisture, and prevent land desertification. Therefore, deforestation significantly lowers living standards for humans and causes health problems.
